THE IMPACT OF FEAR IN GROUP DECISION MAKING


This experiment was designed to closely resemble a management team decision. This experiment was a trail for a new form of experiment designed to assess peopleâ€™s tendencies to act cooperative with their task mates. Primarily, we are interested in whether people display a bias in how they share information and how they take on board new information. This experiment was designed to test the hypothesis that a worry generated from outside the decision-making group increases cooperative tendencies, in contrast, a worry generated from inside the decision-making group decreases cooperative tendencies.

Biases associated with non-cooperative tendencies: a tendency of one to share information that is consistent with their current beliefs & a tendency of one to give a higher weight to information provided by others that is consistent with one's current beliefs.

No method of evaluation of your performance was carried out.
