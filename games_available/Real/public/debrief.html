THE IMPACT OF FEAR IN GROUP DECISION MAKING


This experiment was designed to closely resemble a management team decision and was used to examine how certain cooperative aspects of group decision making (a willingness to adopt other's options and a willingness to share information that opposes one's opinion) change under different forms of task related 'threat'. Here, we predicted that threats applied from outside of the group would cause greater cooperative tendencies and threats originating from inside the group would cause a reduction in cooperative tendencies.

No method of evaluation of your performance was applied.

The experiment was a pilot study for a novel experiment design. The study was a between-subject design. The independent variable was the ‘IMPORTANT NOTE’ section of the task. The dependent variables were metrics signaling: a willingness to adopt other's options and a willingness to share information that opposes one's own opinions.
