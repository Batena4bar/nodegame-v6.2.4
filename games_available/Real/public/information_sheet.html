Participant Information Sheet

About the research

Who will conduct the research?

Mr Samuel Bateman, Alliance Business School - Innovation, Management and Policy Division in The University of Manchester.

What is the purpose of the research?

The purpose of this research is to understand how people make decisions in groups. This research is aimed at developing a new experimental method for studying group decision making.

Participants have been recruited, through the online participant pool Prolific. And have been selected to take part as they meet the necessary English speaking requirements for this study.

Will the outcomes of the research be published?

This research is intended to be used as part of a PhD student thesis in business and management. It is also intended to be published as part of a paper written for a scientific journal.

Who has reviewed the research project?

This study has been reviewed by The Alliance Manchester Business School's Research Ethics Committee.

What are the potential risks?

You may find the task mildly uncomfortable, as is sometimes the case when working collaboratively as part of a group. If you have a history of depression and/or anxiety, then you should not take part in this study.

Your involvement

What would I be asked to do if I took part?

You will be asked to work online as a team comprising other human participants to make decisions in a 'food purchasing' task. You will need to read and discuss information about the options given to identify the best one. Along the way, you and the other participants will be asked to make judgments and decisions about the task and the team. The task should take around 30 minutes from start to finish.

Will I be compensated for taking part?

Each participant will receive a payoff proportional to their group’s performance within the task. The minimum you can receive is £2.50 and the maximum is £7.

Due to the nature of the task, it is not possible to pay partial rewards to participants. Therefore, participants who withdraw from the study will be paid at the base rate of £5/ hour for thier time.

What happens if I do not want to take part or if I change my mind?

It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you do not wish to take part in the study, please close this window and all related windows. If you do decide to take part you are welcome to screenshot this information or download a copy at the end of the experiment. You will also be asked to tick a box to give confirmation of your consent to take part in the study. If you decide to take part you are still free to withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without detriment to yourself (including whilst undertaking the task). However, it is not possible to remove your data from the project once it has been anonymised as we will not be able to identify your specific data. This does not affect your data protection rights. If you decide not to take part you do not need to do anything further.

Data Protection and Confidentiality

What information will you collect about me?

Your “personal identifiable information” will be pulled from the Prolific system. This infomration will not be stored on any local storage devices. Specifically, the information that will be pulled is:

  • Age
  • Gender
  • Country of Residence
  • Education Level
Under what legal basis are you collecting this information?

We are collecting this personal identifiable information in accordance with UK data protection law which protects your rights. These state that we must have a legal basis (specific reason) for collecting your data. For this study, the specific reason is that it is “a public interest task” and “a process necessary for research purposes”.

What are my rights in relation to the information you will collect about me?

You have a number of rights under data protection law regarding your personal information. For example, you can request a copy of the information we hold about you. If you would like to know more about your different rights or the way we use your personal information to ensure we follow the law, please consult our Privacy Notice for Research Participants.

Will my participation in the study be confidential and my personal identifiable information be protected?

In accordance with data protection law, The University of Manchester is the Data Controller for this project. This means that we are responsible for making sure your personal information is kept secure, confidential and used only in the way you have been told it will be used. All researchers are trained with this in mind, and your data will be looked after in the following way:

All personal information will be pseudo anonymised using your Prolific ID. Your Age, Gender, Education Level and Country of Residence, will be pulled from the Prolific website for analysis when needed and will never be held on a storage system local to the resaerch team.

When you agree to take part in a research study and with your informed consent, your anonymised data may be provided to researchers running other studies here or at other organisations. With your consent, your anonymised data will be archived in order to support additional research in accordance with The University of Manchester’s Research Privacy Notice.

This information will not identify you and will not be combined with other information in a way that could identify you. The information will only be used for the purpose of experimentally testing behavioural theory, and cannot be used to contact you. It will not be used to make decisions about future services available to you.

Please also note that individuals from The University of Manchester or regulatory authorities may need to look at the data collected for this study to make sure the project is being carried out as planned. This may involve looking at identifiable data. All individuals involved in auditing and monitoring the study will have a strict duty of confidentiality to you as a research participant.
